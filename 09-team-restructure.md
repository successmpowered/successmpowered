# 09 — Team Section Restructure

**User requirement:** Keep Aditi Sahai and Christopher Greenwood. Replace all other current team members on the site.

This document sets out how to rebuild the team page so that it actively strengthens — rather than currently dilutes — the firm's credibility.

---

## The strategic problem with the current team page

The current About / Team setup lists six people plus two advisors. For a B2B leadership consultancy buyer, this creates three problems:

1. **Small firm, listed like a large one.** Six generalist "directors" reads as a small team overclaiming. Premium buyers are sophisticated — they know a six-person firm isn't running six director-level functions.
2. **Role unclear.** Listing "Director of Training", "Director of Business Development", "Director of Operations" tells the buyer *what these people do inside the business*, not *what they bring to an engagement*. Buyers hire people for what they bring, not for their internal title.
3. **Expertise not surfaced.** The real credentials (legal background, Silicon Valley experience, psychotherapy training, doctorate in natural medicine) are buried in sentence fragments. Every one of those deserves its own line as a credibility asset.
4. **Mixed category signals.** A yoga-nidra wellness specialist on the team page of a leadership consultancy signals category confusion to a CHRO. (This person may be a valuable associate for certain client work — just not on the front-door team page.)

---

## Recommended team architecture

A three-layer structure that matches how premium consultancies actually look.

### Layer 1 — Partners (2)
The people who own the firm, lead engagements, and are accountable to clients.
- **Christopher Greenwood** — Founder & Managing Partner
- **Aditi Sahai** — Partner

### Layer 2 — Associates & Faculty (4–8)
A named faculty of senior, vetted practitioners who bring specialist capability to specific engagements. Each has their own bio. The firm positions them as "our faculty" or "our associate network." This is how serious boutique consultancies scale without inflating their headcount.

### Layer 3 — Operations (not on the public team page)
Anyone running the business behind the scenes (bookings, admin, finance). They exist but they don't appear on the public team page of a professional services firm. This is standard.

## Associate / Faculty layer — what roles to recruit

You currently have four people whose profiles should come off the public team page (Preethi, Sushil, Sanja, Dr Kalpana). Whether they remain involved in the business is entirely a decision for the founders — some may continue as associates, others may not. The team page should focus on who the firm *presents* to clients as its practitioners.

Below is the ideal associate faculty shape. Recruit to fill gaps; position existing contributors who fit these roles appropriately.

### Associate 1 — Senior Executive Coach (UK/Europe)
**Profile:** 15+ years as an executive coach at C-suite level. ICF/EMCC-accredited. Background in corporate leadership before coaching (e.g. ex-operator, not a career coach). Serves the firm's 1:1 coaching engagements with senior executives.

### Associate 2 — Senior Executive Coach (India / Asia)
**Profile:** Senior coach with strong cross-cultural fluency. Ideally with experience in both global multinationals and Indian corporate environments. Covers the firm's coaching engagements in the region and with leaders in cross-border roles.

### Associate 3 — Culture & Organisational Psychology Specialist
**Profile:** Organisational psychologist or experienced culture consultant. Brings diagnostic rigour to culture engagements — data design, measurement, analysis. Likely has a PhD or MSc and experience in large-scale culture work.

### Associate 4 — Complexity & Systems Practitioner
**Profile:** Senior practitioner with experience in complexity-informed leadership and systemic team coaching. Brings edge and depth to engagements where "the usual frameworks" don't hold.

### Associate 5 — Leadership Facilitator & Programme Designer
**Profile:** Expert facilitator of live cohort work, with experience running multi-session leadership programmes for senior audiences. The person who brings the programmes to life in the room.

### Associate 6 — Industry Specialist (optional)
**Profile:** A former senior executive from a strategically important industry (financial services, technology, professional services) who brings industry context to engagements in that sector. Often part-time and engagement-specific.

## Team page design direction

### Structure of the page

1. **Hero** — short section introducing the firm as partner-led.
   - *"Success Empowered is a small, senior team of practitioners. Every engagement is led by a named partner. We bring in associates from our vetted faculty when the work calls for specific expertise."*

2. **The partners** — two large cards side-by-side (Christopher and Aditi). Full-bleed portraits, generous typography. Click-through to individual bio pages.

3. **Our faculty** — grid of 4–8 associate cards. Slightly smaller than partner cards. Each shows portrait, name, one-line specialism. Click-through to individual bio pages.

4. **How we work with associates** — short section explaining the partner-plus-faculty model. Reassures buyers that engagement is always partner-led.
   - *"Every engagement is anchored by a partner — Christopher or Aditi. Our associates join the work where their specific expertise adds most value. This is how we scale depth without compromising it: a small senior team, a vetted faculty, no junior delivery."*

5. **Join our faculty** — footer section for recruiting associates.
   - *"We work with a small number of senior practitioners. If you're experienced in leadership development, coaching, culture work, or organisational psychology and interested in becoming part of our faculty — get in touch."*

### Visual direction for team page

- Commissioned portrait photography, consistent style and lighting across all team members.
- Backgrounds neutral but atmospheric — not white wall, not generic office.
- Consistent aspect ratio for all portraits.
- Names in display typeface, roles in smaller capitalised or small-cap text.
- Bios tightly written — 2 paragraphs maximum on team grid. Full bio lives on individual pages.

## Transition — how to make the change gracefully

Removing four people from a public team page is a sensitive move. A few principles:

- **Have the internal conversation first.** The redesign is a chance to clarify roles going forward — some may remain as associates, some may move on, some may contribute in different ways.
- **Talk to the current team members before launch.** No one should discover their removal from the site by visiting it.
- **Offer alternatives where fit exists.** If someone is genuinely strong in one of the associate slots, offer them a seat in the new faculty structure.
- **Consider timing.** Launch the new team page alongside the broader redesign, not as a standalone change — it draws less attention that way.

---

## Summary

A two-partner, senior-faculty model:
- Is commercially stronger (premium buyers prefer senior leadership on their engagements).
- Is operationally scalable (you add associates to match client work, not overhead).
- Positions Christopher and Aditi correctly (as equal founding partners, not CEO-and-ops).
- Removes category-confusing signals (wellness/yoga at the team level).
- Creates a structural path to bring in the credibility of named, credentialed associates as the business grows.

This is one of the highest-leverage changes in the entire redesign. It costs nothing in cash and changes how the firm reads to every future buyer.
