# 08 — SEO and Messaging Improvements

SEO for a premium leadership consultancy is not about ranking for *"leadership training"* (an impossible, low-quality keyword). It is about being findable by the right buyer when they search for *their specific problem*, and about building authority that compounds over years.

---

## The SEO strategy in one paragraph

Don't chase generic leadership keywords. Own narrow, high-intent keyword territory around specific buyer problems ("how to develop first-time managers", "what to do about middle manager burnout", "how to measure culture change") through high-quality, long-form editorial content published consistently. The compound effect of 24 strong articles over two years is worth far more than any paid campaign.

## Technical SEO

Essentials (not optional for a premium-tier site):

- **Clean semantic HTML.** H1 hierarchy, proper heading structure, descriptive link text.
- **Fast load.** Images compressed to modern formats (AVIF/WebP). Lazy-loading below the fold. Server response <200ms where possible.
- **Mobile-first indexing ready.** Mobile layout must be fully designed, not a squished desktop.
- **Structured data.** Implement Organization, Person (for Christopher and Aditi), and Article schema on Insights pieces.
- **XML sitemap** submitted to Google Search Console.
- **Proper 301 redirects** from any old URLs (important if the rebuild changes URL structure — which it likely will).
- **Canonical tags** on pages with any variation.
- **Open Graph and Twitter Card metadata** on every page. Custom images for social shares.

## Messaging improvements — site-wide voice and language

### Voice principles

1. **Adult.** Don't over-explain. Assume the reader is a senior professional.
2. **Specific.** Replace abstract nouns with concrete scenes or numbers.
3. **Confident, not cocky.** State the firm's position clearly; don't hedge.
4. **Warm, not gushing.** A dry note of humour beats enthusiasm.
5. **British English, international voice.** Grammar defaults to UK; idioms are universal.

### Language substitutions — what to stop saying, what to say instead

| Stop saying | Say instead | Why |
|---|---|---|
| *Empowering people* | *Developing people*, *helping people lead well* | "Empower" is overused to the point of weightlessness |
| *Unlock potential* | *Grow capacity*, *develop judgement* | Cliché |
| *Holistic* | [remove or replace with specifics] | Signals wellness-brand, not consultancy |
| *Transformation journey* | *Engagement*, *programme*, *work* | "Journey" is spiritualised business-speak |
| *Our mission is to...* | *We do X* | Mission-statement language is corporate theatre |
| *Passionate* | [remove entirely] | Used by everyone, distinguishes no one |
| *Thought leadership* | *Writing*, *research*, *notes from our work* | "Thought leadership" signals marketing, not thinking |
| *Awareness tools* | *Ways to understand yourself*, *reflective practice* | Tools-for-awareness reads as jargon |
| *Advanced personal development tools* | [remove or name specifically] | Vague = distrustful |
| *Elevating consciousness* | [remove from site entirely] | Spiritual language, wrong register for B2B |
| *Synergy / alignment / deliverables* | [remove or translate] | Corporate clichés dilute the human voice |

### Taglines — three options to replace the current one

1. **Leadership. Culture. The human work of modern business.**
2. **The partner organisations choose when the leadership work needs to be real.**
3. **Depth. Clarity. Change.**

## Search-friendly positioning without sounding artificial

The principle: write for the reader first, but make the reader's natural phrasing the phrasing of your page.

- If real buyers search for *"leadership programme for new managers"*, that phrase should appear somewhere on the New Manager Programme page — but inside a sentence that reads natural aloud. Don't write *"Our leadership programme for new managers new managers UK bespoke leadership programme for new managers..."* — Google has been past that for a decade.
- **Write the long-form content first.** Then look at it and ask *"what would someone search to find this?"* Edit lightly to align.
- **Use the words the buyer uses,** not the words the industry uses. Buyers say *"middle manager burnout"*. Industry says *"leadership cadre disengagement."*

## The single biggest SEO / messaging move

Publish one genuinely excellent piece of writing under Christopher's byline in month one, and commit to one per month for twelve months. No SEO trick, technical tweak, or keyword list matches the compounding effect of sustained, serious writing from named humans who do this work. Everything else in this document is supporting infrastructure for that central act.
