# 04 — Homepage Redesign

A section-by-section rebuild of the homepage. The layout below is the recommended sequence. Copy is written at a "ready for designer handoff" level — tighten or adjust words, but the structure and sequence should hold.

The homepage is organised around a buyer's reading journey, not a feature list:

1. **Hero** — Orient + provoke enquiry
2. **Proof strip** — Signal credibility in 3 seconds
3. **The problem we solve** — Name the buyer's reality
4. **What we do** — Four service pillars
5. **How we work** — The three-level methodology
6. **Outcomes** — Concrete results
7. **Case study spotlight** — One deep proof artefact
8. **Why this work matters now** — The thesis / contemporary relevance
9. **Who we are** — Team preview with Christopher & Aditi
10. **Closing CTA** — Start a conversation
11. **Footer**

## Section 3 — The problem we solve

This is the section where the buyer reads themselves. Designed to make a CHRO or founder think *"yes, that's exactly our situation."*

### Headline
## The way we work is changing faster than the way we lead.

### Body copy
Hybrid teams. AI reshaping roles. A new generation of managers running people for the first time. Engagement scores falling. Middle managers burning out quietly. Boards asking harder questions about culture.

The organisations that will thrive through the next decade won't be the ones with the best strategy on paper. They'll be the ones with leaders who can genuinely understand themselves, their people, and the moment they're leading in — and cultures that let those leaders do their best work.

That's the work we do.

### Layout direction
Three-column icon row underneath, reinforcing the three symptoms:
- **Leaders stretched beyond their experience** — Promoted quickly, without the foundations to manage people well.
- **Culture slipping quietly** — Engagement, trust, and psychological safety eroding under the weight of change.
- **Strategy outpacing capacity** — The business knows what it needs to do. The people don't yet know how to do it.

## Section 8 — Why this work matters now

A short thesis section. Gives the brand a point of view. Makes the reader feel this is a firm that has *thought* about the moment, not just productised it.

### Headline
## The leadership archetype that got us here won't get us through what's next.

### Body
For most of the last generation, "great leadership" has meant certainty, control, and charisma — a figure at the top who had the answers.

The conditions of modern work don't reward that leader any more. Problems are more complex than one person can hold. Teams are more distributed than one style can reach. Change is faster than one strategy can outlast. AI is rewriting what humans are actually for inside an organisation.

The leaders who will matter over the next decade have a different set of qualities: they know themselves well enough to not be thrown by pressure. They can sit with uncertainty without collapsing into false confidence. They can read people, relationships, and rooms. They can build cultures that bring out the best in others instead of simply broadcasting their own.

That is the capacity we develop.

## Homepage design notes for whoever is building this

- **Above the fold should contain only:** hero (headline, subhead, CTA) + proof strip. No service tiles above the fold. Give the hero room to breathe.
- **Pace the page with rhythm.** Alternate between large editorial sections (one idea, lots of whitespace) and tighter informational sections (cards, grids). Don't let the page become a uniform wall of cards.
- **Every section has one CTA or one onward link maximum.** Don't compete with yourself.
- **Mobile-first.** On mobile this page should read like a well-designed long-form article. Don't cram desktop grids into mobile; collapse them into single-column narrative.
- **Load speed matters for SEO and bounce.** Optimise imagery aggressively. The current site's imagery appears uncompressed in places.
